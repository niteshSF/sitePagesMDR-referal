हिनयानम् - Hīnayāna

The term Hīnayāna (“Lesser Vehicle”) is a later Mahāyāna designation for the earlier schools of Buddhism that emphasized personal liberation through the attainment of Arhatship. It is not a school in itself but a collective reference, often applied to traditions like the Theravāda, though modern scholarship considers the term pejorative. Hīnayāna focuses on the Four Noble Truths, the Eightfold Path, and the cultivation of wisdom and discipline to end individual suffering and attain nirvāṇa. Unlike Mahāyāna, it does not stress the Bodhisattva ideal, but rather the goal of freeing oneself from the cycle of birth and death. Despite the label, the so-called Hīnayāna schools laid the foundational teachings and practices of Buddhism, which later Mahāyāna and Vajrayāna traditions built upon.
