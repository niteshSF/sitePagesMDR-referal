माध्यमिक - Mādhyamika

The Mādhyamika school, founded by the great philosopher Nāgārjuna in the 2nd century CE, is one of the most influential philosophical traditions within Mahāyāna. It is based on the concept of śūnyatā (emptiness), which asserts that all phenomena lack intrinsic existence and arise only through dependent origination (pratītyasamutpāda). By dismantling extremes of eternalism and nihilism, Mādhyamika presents a “middle way” that avoids rigid conceptions of reality. Through its method of dialectical reasoning, the school shows how clinging to fixed notions leads to suffering, while understanding emptiness opens the path to true wisdom and liberation. Its subtle insights became foundational for later Buddhist traditions across Tibet, China, and beyond.
