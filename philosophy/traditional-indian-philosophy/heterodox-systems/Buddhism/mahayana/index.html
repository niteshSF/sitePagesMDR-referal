महायानम् - Mahāyāna

The Mahāyāna or “Great Vehicle” branch of Buddhism emerged around the 1st century BCE and expanded widely across Central, East, and Southeast Asia. It emphasizes the Bodhisattva ideal, where practitioners aspire to attain Buddhahood for the sake of liberating all sentient beings rather than seeking personal nirvāṇa alone. Central to Mahāyāna are the virtues of compassion (karuṇā) and wisdom (prajñā), along with the cultivation of pāramitās (perfections) such as generosity, morality, patience, energy, meditation, and insight. Its rich philosophical and devotional traditions include a vast corpus of sūtras, veneration of celestial Buddhas and Bodhisattvas, and the vision of ultimate reality as boundless and accessible to all.
