सौत्रान्तिक - Sautrāntika

The Sautrāntika was an early Buddhist school belonging to the Hīnayāna tradition, flourishing around the 2nd century BCE to 4th century CE. The name means “those who rely on the sūtras,” indicating their emphasis on the original Buddhist discourses (sūtras) over the later Abhidharma treatises. Philosophically, they are known for a realistic and momentarist view of existence, holding that only present mental and physical events (dharmas) are real, while past and future phenomena exist only as latent impressions. They also developed the theory of seeds (bīja) within the mind to explain how karmic actions produce future results, a concept that later influenced Yogācāra thought. By stressing direct perception and scriptural authority, the Sautrāntikas stood as critics of the Sarvāstivāda school, while still shaping key debates in Buddhist philosophy.
