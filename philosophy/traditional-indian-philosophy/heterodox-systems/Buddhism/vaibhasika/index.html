वैभाषिक - Vaibhāṣika

The Vaibhāṣika school, a prominent branch of the Sarvāstivāda tradition, developed a highly systematic philosophy based on the Mahāvibhāṣā Śāstra compiled around the 2nd century CE in Kashmir. Its central doctrine is sarvāsti-vāda—the view that all dharmas (phenomena) exist in the past, present, and future, though only the present is causally active. Vaibhāṣikas emphasized the authority of the Abhidharma texts and classified reality into an elaborate system of ultimate constituents, aiming to provide a precise analysis of existence and the path to liberation. They also asserted the reality of external objects, differing from the later Sautrāntikas, who emphasized mental impressions. With their detailed scholasticism and reliance on Abhidharma, the Vaibhāṣikas played a key role in shaping Buddhist metaphysics and debates that later influenced Mahāyāna thinkers like Nāgārjuna.
