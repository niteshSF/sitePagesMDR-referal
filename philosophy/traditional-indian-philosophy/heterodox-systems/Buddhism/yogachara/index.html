योगाचार - Yogācāra

The Yogācāra, also known as the “School of Consciousness” or “Mind-Only” (Cittamātra), is another major philosophical tradition within Mahāyāna Buddhism, flourishing around the 4th–5th century CE through the works of Asaṅga and Vasubandhu. It teaches that all experiences are shaped by consciousness, and what we perceive as external reality is essentially a projection of the mind. Central to its thought is the doctrine of the ālaya-vijñāna (storehouse consciousness), a deep layer of mind where karmic seeds are stored and from which perceptions arise. Yogācāra emphasizes meditation, introspection, and mental discipline (yoga) as the path to purify these latent impressions and realize ultimate truth. By highlighting the constructive role of consciousness, it complements Mādhyamika’s philosophy of emptiness, offering a more psychological and experiential framework for understanding liberation.
