Heterodox systems

The Heterodox (nāstika) systems of Indian philosophy are those that do not accept the authority of the Vedas, though they offer profound insights into ethics, metaphysics, and liberation. Buddhism teaches the Four Noble Truths, the Middle Path, and the doctrine of momentariness and no-self; Jainism emphasizes ahiṃsā (non-violence), anekāntavāda (many-sided truth), and ascetic discipline as the way to mokṣa; while the Cārvāka (Lokāyata) school represents materialism, rejecting afterlife and karma, and advocating direct perception as the only valid means of knowledge. Despite their rejection of Vedic authority, these systems significantly shaped India’s intellectual, spiritual, and cultural landscape, often engaging in dialogue with the orthodox traditions.
