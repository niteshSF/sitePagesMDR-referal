Orthodox Systems

The orthodox (āstika) systems of Indian philosophy are six classical schools that accept the authority of the Vedas while offering diverse perspectives on reality, knowledge, and liberation. Nyāya emphasizes logic and epistemology; Vaiśeṣika analyzes the categories of existence through an atomistic view; Sāṅkhya presents a dualism of puruṣa (consciousness) and prakṛti (matter); Yoga, closely allied with Sāṅkhya, provides a practical path of discipline and meditation for liberation; Pūrva Mīmāṃsā upholds Vedic ritual and dharma as the means to harmony; and Vedānta (Uttara Mīmāṃsā) explores the nature of Brahman, the self, and ultimate reality, with schools like Advaita, Viśiṣṭādvaita, and Dvaita offering varied interpretations. Together, they form the core of the āstika tradition, balancing reason, ritual, and spirituality.
