न्याय - Nyāya

The Nyāya system is one of the six orthodox (āstika) schools of Indian philosophy, founded by Gautama (Akṣapāda). It is primarily concerned with logic, epistemology, and the means of valid knowledge (pramāṇa). Nyāya recognizes four pramāṇas: pratyakṣa (perception), anumāna (inference), upamāna (comparison/analogy), and śabda (verbal testimony of reliable authorities). The school developed a detailed system of logic to test the validity of arguments and to distinguish truth from error, forming the foundation of classical Indian logical reasoning. Although Nyāya emphasizes rational inquiry, it is not merely intellectual — its ultimate goal, like other darśanas, is mokṣa (liberation), achieved by removing false knowledge and attaining true understanding of reality and the self.
