द्वैत - Dvaita
(Madhvacharya)

Dvaita, expounded by Śrī Madhvācārya, is a philosophy of dualism that upholds an eternal and unbridgeable distinction between Brahman (Viṣṇu), the supreme independent reality, and the dependent realities of the individual souls (jīvas) and matter (prakṛti). In this vision, God alone is infinite and self-sufficient, while all else exists only through His will. Unlike Advaita, which sees liberation as identity with Brahman, Dvaita teaches that mokṣa is eternal blissful service at the feet of Viṣṇu, attained through unwavering bhakti and His grace. Souls are understood to have gradations, yet each finds its ultimate fulfillment in devotion, where the relationship of the soul with God is never dissolved but cherished as eternally distinct.
