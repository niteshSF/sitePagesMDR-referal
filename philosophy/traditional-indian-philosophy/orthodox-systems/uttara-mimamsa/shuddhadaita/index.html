शुद्धाद्वैत - Śuddhādvaita
(Vallabhacharya)

Śuddhādvaita, expounded by Śrī Vallabhācārya, teaches the doctrine of “pure non-dualism”, where the world is not regarded as illusion (māyā) but as a real manifestation of Brahman. According to this view, everything in existence is Brahman expressing itself in diverse forms, and hence creation is intrinsically divine. The path to liberation is not through renunciation but through loving devotion (puṣṭi-bhakti) to Śrī Kṛṣṇa, particularly in his child form as Śrīnāthjī. The devotee experiences union with the Divine by embracing the world as God’s play (līlā) and offering one’s life in joyous service and love.
