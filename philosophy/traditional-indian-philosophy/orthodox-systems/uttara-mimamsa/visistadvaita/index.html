विशिष्टाद्वैत - Viśiṣṭādvaita
(Ramanujacharya)

Vishishtadvaita, systematized by Rāmānuja, is a prominent school of Vedānta philosophy emphasizing “qualified non-dualism.” It asserts that the individual soul (jīva) and the universe are real and inseparable parts of Brahman, the ultimate reality. Unlike Advaita, which sees the world as illusion, Vishishtadvaita sees the world as a real manifestation of Brahman. Liberation (mokṣa) is attained through devotion (bhakti), knowledge (jñāna), and righteous action (karma), emphasizing a personal relationship with God and the unity of all beings within the divine whole.
