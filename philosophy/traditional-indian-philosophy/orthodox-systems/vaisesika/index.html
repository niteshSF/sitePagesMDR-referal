वैशेषिक - Vaiśeṣika

The Vaiśeṣika system, founded by Kaṇāda (Kaṇabhuk), is closely related to Nyāya but focuses more on metaphysics and natural philosophy. It explains the world through categories called padārthas (things that can be named and known). The classical list includes six: dravya (substance), guṇa (quality), karman (action), sāmānya (universal), viśeṣa (particularity), and samavāya (inherence); later texts add abhāva (non-existence). At its core, Vaiśeṣika proposes an atomic theory of the universe — all physical objects are composed of eternal, indivisible atoms (aṇu) that combine in various ways. It also accepts the existence of the soul (ātman), God (Īśvara), and karma as laws governing the cosmos. Like Nyāya, its ultimate aim is mokṣa, achieved through true knowledge of reality, but its distinctive contribution is a systematic classification of the physical and metaphysical world.
