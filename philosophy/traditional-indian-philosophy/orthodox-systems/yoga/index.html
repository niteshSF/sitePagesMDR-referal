योग - Yoga

The Yoga system, codified by Patañjali in the Yoga Sūtras, is closely allied with Sāṃkhya but adds the dimension of practice and discipline. While Sāṃkhya provides the theoretical framework of Puruṣa (consciousness) and Prakṛti (matter), Yoga teaches the practical means to realize the separation of the two and thereby attain kaivalya (liberation). Patañjali outlines the Aṣṭāṅga Yoga — the Eight Limbs of Yoga: yama (ethical restraints), niyama (observances), āsana (postures), prāṇāyāma (breath control), pratyāhāra (withdrawal of senses), dhāraṇā (concentration), dhyāna (meditation), and samādhi (absorption). These steps guide the aspirant from external discipline to inner realization. Unlike classical Sāṃkhya, Yoga accepts the existence of Īśvara (a special puruṣa, or God), who aids the yogin through devotion and meditation. The Yoga system thus integrates philosophy with spiritual practice, aiming at mastery over body, mind, and senses to reveal the pure, liberated self.
