The art of encryption, or Küțalekhanam, was a sophisticated practice that blended linguistic creativity, mathematics, and strategic foresight. Rooted in texts like the Arthaśāstra, encryption methods included coded language, metaphors, and steganography, ensuring secure communication in warfare, diplomacy, and personal interactions. The Kațapayādi system exemplifies how numerical values were encoded into language, highlighting the mathematical depth behind these techniques. These early encryption practices, influencing both military strategy and personal exchanges, laid the foundation for modern cryptographic methods and underscore Bhārat's enduring intellectual legacy.
