Ayurveda, one of the oldest holistic healing systems in the world, reflects the profound wisdom and ingenuity of ancient Bharat. Rooted in the Vedic tradition, Ayurveda-meaning "science of life"-offers a comprehensive approach to health by integrating physical, mental, and spiritual practices. Tracing its origins to the Vedas, particularly the Atharvaveda, Ayurveda encompasses the works of the Brhat Trayi, including the Caraka Samhitä, Suśruta Samhita, and Astänga Hrdaya, which provide insights into medicine, surgery, and pharmacology. Central to this tradition is the concept of dosas, which govern human physiology and health. Ayurveda is also renowned for its extensive use of herbal medicine, innovative surgical practices, and spiritual methodologies, such as yoga and meditation, highlighting its holistic view of well-being. As a living tradition with global influence, Ayurveda continues to inspire modern healthcare, emphasizing balance, personalized care, and the interconnectedness of body, mind and spirit.
