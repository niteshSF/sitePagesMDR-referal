Nādi Vijñāna, the ancient science of pulse diagnosis in traditional Bhāratiya medicine, embodies a holistic approach to understanding the physical, mental, and emotional states of individuals through the non-invasive art of pulse examination, known as Nādi Parīkṣā. This sacred practice allows experienced Ayurvedic practitioners, or Vaidyas, to diagnose imbalances in the body's doșas -Vāta, Pitta and Kapha-by analyzing the pulse at specific points on the wrist, reflecting the deep interconnectedness of body, mind, and spirit. Historically, Nādī Parīkṣā has been documented in revered texts like the Särngadhara Samhitā and Yogaratnākara, which detail its methodology and significance in assessing not just physical ailments but also mental and emotional well-being. With modern innovations like the Nādi Tarangiņi, which integrates technology with traditional techniques, Nādi Vijñāna is being revitalized, ensuring its relevance in contemporary health practices and demonstrating its potential to address modern health challenges effectively.
